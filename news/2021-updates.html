Some Major Changes

09 December, 2020 //

It’s safe to say that 2020 has been a chaotic and unexpected year for pretty much everybody around the world. Although I’m completely aware not many can agree, I’ve found 2020 to be one of the best years of my life thus far, despite everything it’s thrown my way.

At the beginning of this year, I had intentions of making a second game, Hot Doggerie, and the quarantine over March gave me the opportunity to pick up learning Python again through developing Morgann for a few friends. Isolation also gave me way too much time to work on other projects, so I ended up picking up web development as well for my own site.
Also during this year, I began development of my second bot, Brandonn, which I have been developing since announcing in August.

Moving into 2021, however, I’d like to shift my focus.

I will no longer be actively developing Hot Doggerie or Brandonn, and there’s a few reasons for this. The largest reason for this is I don’t enjoy massive projects (although I have a tendency to pick them up all too often).
There are certainly more reasons as to why I chose to do this, and I have written case studies for both Hot Doggerie and Brandonn detailing my experiences and more.

So what am I wanting to do? For a start, I would like to revisit some old projects. Morgann was loved among my personal friend group, and I’m currently working on a major update to rework his core features using the skills I learned from developing Brandonn, and add some neat new features.
Another project I want to revisit (note this is highly subjective to how I feel after pre-development) Gravity. I left Gravity on a somewhat disappointing note, and I believe I may be able to dramatically improve the game with a refreshed interface, refined mechanics, and new features.
Suffice it to say, I have many ideas I’d like to work on for both of those projects, and I’ll be releasing more information about them as I work on them.

In terms of new projects, I would like to keep to smaller, 1-2 month projects that I won’t get too bored of, but will still give me the opportunity to learn something. Here’s a few of my general spitball ideas, most of which I’m going to put here for future reference if I’m bored:

  • An archive project: mostly to store old & outdated project case studies for anyone feeling nostalgic (like me), I’d like to have a subdomain (like archive.isaacshea.com) that would store a year-by-year record of all my writings and whatnot.
  • The Artisan: a few months ago I redesigned my girlfriend’s custom app icons on her phone because I hated the ones she had, but I enjoyed making (and looking at) them so much that I’d love to share that in a neat website. It may also be a bit far-fetched, but I’d like to make my own, not garbage-looking widget app to complement it.

Despite my current ideas of what I want to do, I make no guarantee that I will get around to, or finish, developing these projects. I’d like to at this stage, but this year has shown anyone that plans need to be somewhat flexible.
Also in the new year I will be starting my first year of university, so I’m yet to see how that will affect my workflow and the time I have to dedicate to these projects.

To everyone who is interested in my work, whether that be family, friends, or complete strangers, thank you! It’s nice to have an outlet for my progress and thoughts on development, and I am looking to post updates more regularly this year.
